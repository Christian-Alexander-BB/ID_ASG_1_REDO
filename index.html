Our Objective

At SpaceX, we develop bleeding-edge rocket technologies to take machines, and eventually humans, to space. We are the first company to have a rocket that can land land vertically and be reused, the first private company to send humans to the International Space Station, the first private company to launch something to orbit the sun, and many more.

Since Appollo 11, interest in space exploration has been declining. Less and less people started to look to the stars with awe. Here, we hope to reignite the spark that is outer space. Our ultimate goal is to make space travel available to almost anyone and make space exploration exciting for the public again.

Mission To Mars And Beyond

Our largest goal is to make humanity an interplanetary species. The first step in doing this is by colonizing Mars, the nearest habitable planet to Earth. Once we can survive - and possibly thrive - on Mars, we can work on inhabiting other planets further away from Earth.

Reusable Rockets

Traditionally, launch vehicles like boosters need to be repaired or rebuilt, making them non-reusable and expensive. However, SpaceX launch vehicles are the first of its kind. They can land vertically and be reused within a short period of time. Our Falcon 9 rocket pioneered this technology.

Starlink

With more than 800 satellites working together in low earth orbit, Starlink will provide fast internet to virtually anywhere on Earth. This system will be unbounded by the download and upload speed limit of traditional satellite internet, nor ground infrastructure which prevents internet via cabling.
